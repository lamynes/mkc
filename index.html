Mobile Kennel Club, Inc. est. 1921

The Mobile Kennel Club is an all-breed dog club, licensed by the American Kennel Club. It is a non-profit organization, established in 1921, to further the advancement of Purebred dogs. The purpose of the Mobile Kennel Club is to promote Purebred dogs through proper care and careful breeding. This club plans practice matches, dog shows etc. Our members represent a wide variety of breeds and many of our members actively participate in AKC competition events including, conformation, obedience, agility, earth dog, hunt tests, herding instinct, scent work, and more! The Mobile Kennel Club provides educational information for interested dog owners and enthusiasts through programs at our monthly meetings.

A whole new world of family activities

Join us for fun and educational family activities with your dog. We love welcoming new members to our club and showing them the variety of activities they can enjoy with their canine companions. We also encourage young children to participate and learn how to care for their dogs responsibly. Our club meetings are open to everyone who loves dogs. We can also offer guidance on choosing the right breed of dog for your family's lifestyle.

Our Meetings

Meetings are open to the public the first Wednesday of every month @7 PM at the IHOP 4375 Rangeline Rd, Tillmans Corner (Mobile) Alabama. (in the conference room to the right as you enter the building) Come earlier if you plan to order dinner.

How many breeds of dog are recognized by The Kennel Club?

  • The Kennel Club recognizes 222 dog breeds.
  •  The United Kennel Club (UKC) recognizes over 300 breeds.
  • The American Kennel Club (AKC) recognizes 200 breeds.
  •  The Continental Kennel Club recognizes over 450 purebred dogs.
